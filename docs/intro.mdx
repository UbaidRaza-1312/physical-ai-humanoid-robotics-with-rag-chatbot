Welcome to the **Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics Textbook**!

This textbook is designed to be a comprehensive and academically rigorous resource covering the principles of Physical AI, Humanoid Robotics, and the intricate process of integrating AI systems into physical environments. Our core purpose is to bridge the gap between theoretical digital AI concepts and their practical application in the real world, specifically focusing on humanoid robots.

## Project Goal

The primary goal of this textbook is to empower students and researchers to understand, design, simulate, and deploy AI-powered humanoid robots. You will learn how AI systems function in reality and understand the physical laws that govern embodied intelligence.

## Target Audience

This textbook is specifically tailored for:
-   **Undergraduate or graduate students** in computer science, robotics, or AI.
-   **Educators and researchers** interested in the cutting edge of Physical AI and humanoid robotics.

A foundational understanding of computer science and basic robotics concepts is recommended.

## Focus and Theme

Our central theme is **Embodied Intelligence** – the study and development of AI systems that exist and interact within the physical world. The textbook will guide you through the process of applying AI knowledge to control humanoid robots in both simulated and real-world environments.

## Quarter Overview

The textbook is structured into a logical progression of modules, designed to take you from foundational concepts to advanced integrations and a culminating capstone project:

-   **Introduction to Physical AI**: Understanding AI systems that operate in reality and their adherence to physical laws.
-   **Design, Simulate, and Deploy**: Hands-on experience using key tools like ROS 2, Gazebo, Unity, and NVIDIA Isaac.
-   **Capstone Project**: An autonomous humanoid robot performing complex, multi-modal tasks, integrating all learned concepts.

## Modules

The textbook is divided into four main modules, each building upon the last:

1.  **Module 1: The Robotic Nervous System (ROS 2)**
    *   Learn ROS 2 fundamentals for robot control, including nodes, topics, services, actions, and Python integration (rclpy). Understand URDF for humanoid modeling.
2.  **Module 2: The Digital Twin (Gazebo & Unity)**
    *   Explore physics simulation (gravity, collisions) in Gazebo and high-fidelity visualization in Unity. Learn sensor simulation and synchronization techniques.
3.  **Module 3: The AI-Robot Brain (NVIDIA Isaac)**
    *   Dive into NVIDIA Isaac Sim for photorealistic simulation, synthetic dataset generation, VSLAM, navigation (Nav2), and reinforcement learning for robot control.
4.  **Module 4: Vision-Language-Action (VLA) & Capstone**
    *   Integrate Large Language Models (LLMs) with robotics, using OpenAI Whisper for natural language commands and cognitive planning to translate commands into ROS 2 action sequences. Conclude with a capstone project involving an autonomous humanoid performing multi-modal tasks.
